# Sources — Sleep Music for Infants and Young Children

Curated, vetted source list for `research/sleep-music-research.md`.
Only reputable scientific / medical / public-health publications and bodies.
Last verified: 2026-06.

## Tier 1 — Peer-reviewed, directly on topic (infants / NICU / pediatric)

1. **Hugh SC, Wolf MJ, Beauchamp KA, Bondoc L, Yeh P, Anderson PJ.** *Infant sleep machines and hazardous sound pressure levels.* **Pediatrics.** 2014 Apr;133(4):677-81. doi: 10.1542/peds.2013-3617. PMID: 24590753.
   - PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24590753/
   - DOI: https://doi.org/10.1542/peds.2013-3617
   - Quote (abstract): "Maximum sound levels at 30 cm were >50 A-weighted dB for all devices… Three machines produced output levels >85 A-weighted dB, which, if played at these levels for >8 hours, exceeds current occupational limits…"

2. **Loewy J, Stewart K, Dassler AM, Telsey A, Homel P.** *The effects of music therapy on vital signs, feeding, and sleep in premature infants.* **Pediatrics.** 2013 May;131(5):902-18. doi: 10.1542/peds.2012-1367. PMID: 23589814.
   - PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23589814/
   - DOI: https://doi.org/10.1542/peds.2012-1367
   - Quote (abstract): "Lower heart rates occurred during the lullaby (P < .001) and rhythm intervention (P = .04)… Caloric intake (P = .01) and sucking behavior (P = .02) were higher with parent-preferred lullabies."

3. **Sibrecht G, Borys F, Campone C, Bruschettini M, Soll R.** *Noise or sound management in the neonatal intensive care unit for preterm or very low birth weight infants.* **Cochrane Database Syst Rev.** 2024 May 30;5(5):CD010333. doi: 10.1002/14651858.CD010333.pub4. PMID: 38813836.
   - PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38813836/

4. **Puyana-Romero V, Núñez-Solano D, Hernández-Molina R, Jara-Muñoz E.** *Influence of the NICU on the Acoustic Isolation of a Neonatal Incubator.* **Front Pediatr.** 2020 Sep 22;8:588. doi: 10.3389/fped.2020.00588. PMID: 33072664.
   - PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33072664/
   - Quote (abstract): "international guidelines suggest avoiding noise levels above 45 dB in NICUs…"

5. **Liao JH, Hu RF, Su LJ, Wang S, Xu Q, Qian XF, He HG.** *Nonpharmacological Interventions for Sleep Promotion on Preterm Infants in NICU: A Systematic Review.* **Worldviews Evid Based Nurs.** 2018 Oct;15(5):386-393. doi: 10.1111/wvn.12315. PMID: 30098111.
   - PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30098111/

6. **Lai HL, Chen CJ, Peng TC, Chang FM, Hsieh ML, Huang HY, Chang SC.** *Randomized controlled trial of music during kangaroo care on maternal state anxiety and preterm infants' responses.* **Int J Nurs Stud.** 2006 Feb;43(2):139-46. doi: 10.1016/j.ijnurstu.2005.04.008. PMID: 15996669.

7. **Dearn T, Shoemark H.** *The effect of maternal presence on premature infant response to recorded music.* **J Obstet Gynecol Neonatal Nurs (JOGNN).** 2014 May-Jun;43(3):341-50. doi: 10.1111/1552-6909.12303. PMID: 24707819.

## Tier 2 — Authoritative public-health / guideline bodies

9. **NIH/NIDCD.** *Noise-Induced Hearing Loss (NIHL).* National Institute on Deafness and Other Communication Disorders.
   - URL: https://www.nidcd.nih.gov/health/noise-induced-hearing-loss
   - Quote: "Sounds at or below 70 A-weighted decibels (dBA), even after long exposure, are unlikely to cause hearing loss. However, long or repeated exposure to sounds at or above 85 dBA can cause hearing loss."

10. **World Health Organization.** *Deafness and hearing loss* (Fact Sheet).
    - URL: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/deafness-and-hearing-loss
    - Quote: "Over 1 billion young adults are at risk of permanent, avoidable hearing loss due to unsafe listening practices."

11. **American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP)** — referenced indirectly via Pediatrics journal articles (Hugh 2014, Loewy 2013) and AAP-cited NICU noise guidance (≤ 45 dB Leq). General AAP publications hub:
    - URL: https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics

## Notes on source quality

- All Tier 1 entries are peer-reviewed and indexed on PubMed.
- *Pediatrics* is the flagship AAP journal; multiple entries here are from it.
- *Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews* and *Frontiers in Pediatrics* are recognized higher-tier outlets.
- NIH/NIDCD and WHO are used only for normative SPL safety thresholds and public-health framing, not for infant-specific music claims.
- No parenting blogs, SEO content, marketing pages, or unverifiable "list" articles were used.
