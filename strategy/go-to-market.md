# Go-To-Market

## Market Entry Strategy

AI Lullaby Radio should enter the market as a trust-led bedtime utility, not as an AI novelty. The first users should understand that the product is deliberately constrained: quiet, slow, predictable, timer-first, and cautious about safety claims.

The initial launch should stay narrow:

- Web-first MVP.
- Small controlled catalog.
- Clear safety and limitation messaging.
- Feedback from parents before large-scale promotion.

## Core Message

"A calm, controlled lullaby radio for bedtime routines."

Supporting points:

- Designed to reduce sudden musical surprises.
- Uses quiet defaults and sleep timers.
- Prioritizes stable mood, tempo, and loudness.
- Built for parents who do not want to monitor a playlist after bedtime starts.

Avoid:

- "Makes babies sleep."
- "Improves child development."
- "Clinically proven sleep therapy."
- "Safe at any volume all night."

## Audience Segments

### Parents Of Infants

Need: gentle, safe, low-effort sleep-onset support.

Message: quiet bedtime background with timer-first playback and safety guidance.

### Parents Of Toddlers

Need: predictable evening routine and fewer stimulating surprises.

Message: a consistent musical environment that does not turn into daytime playlist energy.

### Sleep-Sensitive Adults

Need: low-stimulation audio without algorithmic surprises.

Message: controlled calm radio for rest, reading, and sleep onset.

## Feedback Collection

Start with qualitative feedback before scaling traffic.

Recommended channels:

- In-product post-session prompt with 3-5 simple buttons.
- Parent interview form after 3 uses.
- Manual feedback link on the landing page.
- Small private test group with 5-10 parents.

High-signal questions:

- Did you need to adjust volume after starting?
- Did any transition attract attention?
- Did any track feel too bright, loud, rhythmic, or emotional?
- Did the timer length match the bedtime routine?
- Would you trust this product without monitoring the queue?

Feedback should map directly to track metadata and session behavior, not only generic satisfaction.

## SEO Strategy

The SEO strategy should be educational and safety-aware. Search content should answer practical questions parents already ask, while making clear that the product is not medical advice.

Priority clusters:

- "lullaby music for sleep"
- "baby sleep music volume"
- "is white noise safe for babies"
- "sleep timer music for kids"
- "calm bedtime music without ads"
- "lullabies without sudden loud sounds"
- "how loud should baby sleep music be"

Content principles:

- Cite conservative safety ranges from the research notes.
- Explain why normal playlists can be unsuitable for sleep.
- Include disclaimers for medical concerns and hearing safety.
- Promote quiet, brief, timer-based use.
- Avoid fabricated clinical certainty.

Initial content pieces:

- Why bedtime music should be quieter than you think.
- Why playlists can fail at bedtime.
- Sleep timer vs. all-night playback.
- What makes a lullaby low-stimulation.
- How to place a speaker safely in a child's room.

## Habr As A Channel

Habr is useful for technical trust and early adopter credibility, not direct parent conversion alone.

Recommended angle:

- "How we built a controlled sleep-radio MVP instead of another music recommender."

Topics to cover:

- Why engagement-optimized recommendation is a poor fit for bedtime.
- The 18-track local catalog and why small controlled catalogs are acceptable at MVP stage.
- Audio constraints: loudness, tempo, transitions, timbre.
- Frontend-only MVP architecture.
- Safety and claim limitations.
- Planned audio QA pipeline.

What not to do on Habr:

- Do not overclaim sleep outcomes.
- Do not present AI generation as solved quality.
- Do not hide catalog limitations.
- Do not turn the post into medical advice.

Success metrics for Habr:

- Comments with technical or parent-use feedback.
- Referral traffic to landing page.
- Newsletter or waitlist signups.
- Suggestions for audio QA tooling, dataset design, or safety checks.

## Launch Sequence

### Private Alpha

- 5-10 parents or caregivers.
- Current 18-track catalog.
- Manual feedback collection.
- No paid acquisition.

### Public MVP Page

- Landing page with app link.
- Safety disclaimer and clear product limitations.
- Feedback form.
- Analytics only if privacy notice is ready.

### Technical Story

- Habr article after the MVP can be demonstrated.
- Link to the product, safety notes, and roadmap.
- Ask for feedback on audio QA and playlist constraints.

### Search Content

- Publish practical articles based on the safety and product docs.
- Use internal links to the app and safety guidance.
- Update content when audio QA becomes automated.
